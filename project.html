Autonomous Vehicle Sensor Suite

SolidWorks CAD Waterproofing DFM FEA Sensor Integration Testing

Project Overview

Sensor Suite V3 is a modular, compact sensor mounting solution optimized for autonomous vehicle applications. The redesign focused on accurate sensor positioning, integrated waterproofing, and a localized power delivery system to reduce cabling complexity.

Mechanical Design & Manufacturing

The project began with a full mechanical redesign focused on modularity and compactness, using CAD tools to ensure precise sensor alignment and ease of assembly. Each sensor was carefully positioned to maximize field of view and minimize blind spots, with vibration-dampening mounts implemented for the LiDAR units to improve data fidelity. Waterproofing was achieved through gasketed enclosures, sealed connectors, and the use of corrosion-resistant materials. A localized power delivery system was integrated directly into the suite to simplify the wiring architecture and reduce the number of long cables routed throughout the vehicle.

Performance & Results

The final Sensor Suite V3 significantly improved robustness, ease of installation, and system reliability. The integrated waterproofing successfully protected electronics during outdoor testing in wet conditions. The localized power system reduced cable length by over 50%, minimizing signal noise and easing maintenance. The modular design allowed for quick sensor swaps and streamlined debugging, which accelerated development cycles. Overall, the redesign enhanced the vehicle’s sensing capabilities while making the suite more maintainable, scalable, and competition-ready.

Crank Slider Mobile Robot

SolidWorks Linkage Kinematics Motion Studies 3D Printing Laser Cutting PID Control Odometry

Project Overview

Design and fabricate a two-wheeled differential-drive robot capable of autonomous navigation and precise object manipulation using a custom crank-slider arm mechanism. The project balanced mechanical design, fabrication constraints, and control systems integration.

Mechanical Design & Fabrication

All mechanical components were designed in SolidWorks, with linkage kinematics validated through motion studies before fabrication. The differential drive base, custom crank-slider arm, and all structural components were fabricated via 3D printing and laser cutting, enabling rapid iteration. This design-to-fabrication pipeline proved critical for achieving tight mechanical tolerances and optimizing the arm's reach and torque characteristics.

Motion studies in SolidWorks validated linkage kinematics and actuation profiles before fabrication. Clearances and component fit were verified through 3D printing prototypes. The integrated encoder-based odometry and PID motor control on a Raspberry Pi Pico W enabled coordinated motion between drive and manipulation systems. Functional testing under load conditions drove iterative mechanical refinements and control parameter tuning.

Integration & Results

Integrated encoder-based odometry and PID motor control enabled coordinated motion between drive and manipulation systems. Functional testing under load conditions drove iterative refinements to both mechanical design and control parameters. The final system achieved top 10 competition placement in a class of 80+ students, validating the design approach of rigorous mechanical engineering combined with disciplined control system development.

Competition Results

The robot successfully demonstrated autonomous navigation and reliable object manipulation during final competition, resulting in a top 10 finish in a class of 80+ students. The robust mechanical design and consistent autonomous performance exemplifies how solid mechanical fundamentals—precise fabrication, iterative validation, and thoughtful integration—form the foundation for successful autonomous systems.

Automatic Squirrel Feeder

SolidWorks Stepper Motor Archimedes Screw Vision System YOLO ML Force Sensor DFM

Project Overview

The Automated Squirrel Feeder is a precision dispensing system designed for backyard wildlife management, combining mechanical innovation and intelligent control. The project features a stepper-driven Archimedes screw for accurate portion control, a vision-assisted system using YOLO ML for animal identification, and a force-sensitive resistor for closed-loop feedback. The enclosure was engineered to prevent jamming and accommodate pellet variability, with iterative prototyping and testing to optimize reliability and precision.

Mechanical & Electrical Design

The feeder mechanism was developed through CAD modeling and simulation, focusing on minimizing friction and ensuring robust operation. The system integrates a stepper motor, force sensor, and vision system, all coordinated by a microcontroller. The electronics were selected for durability and ease of integration, with waterproofing and weatherproofing measures implemented for outdoor use. The design report details the engineering process, including material selection, stress analysis, and iterative testing to validate performance under real-world conditions.

Results & Achievements

The final system demonstrated reliable portion dispensing, effective animal identification, and robust operation in outdoor environments. The project finished 1st overall in a competitive design course, validating both the mechanical design and system integration. The design report highlights the importance of iterative prototyping, simulation, and real-world testing in achieving a successful engineering solution for wildlife management.

Modular Cycloidal Gearbox

SolidWorks Cycloidal Drive 3D Printing CAD FEA DFM

Project Overview

Designed and 3D printed a compact 20:1 cycloidal reduction gearbox as a modular actuator component, optimizing for high torque amplification with minimal backlash for precision robotic applications. The exploded view video demonstrates the internal mechanism and assembly process. Technical documentation and demo video are available for further details.

Design & Optimization

The design focused on critical cycloidal mechanism parameters: eccentric offset geometry, pin profile tolerancing, and disc geometry optimization to achieve the target 20:1 reduction ratio while balancing torque capacity and efficiency. Modeled all contacts, clearances, and bearing interfaces in SolidWorks to ensure proper fit and function.

Testing & Application

Conducted iterative physical testing to evaluate torque efficiency, backlash performance, wear patterns, and failure modes. Used test results to refine disc geometry, pin tolerances, and material selection. This hands-on validation approach ensured the gearbox would perform reliably when integrated into a custom robotic arm system. The gearbox is being developed as a modular actuator component for future integration into a robotic arm, demonstrating the importance of precision mechanical design and rigorous testing in motion control applications.

Autonomous Mobile Robot

SolidWorks ROS2 SLAM 3D Printing Power Distribution Sensor Integration Prototyping

Project Overview

Designed and fabricated a compact, general-purpose autonomous mobile robot from the ground up, integrating chassis, drivetrain, power distribution, and sensor systems. The robot utilizes a Raspberry Pi, Arduino, and Lidar sensors for autonomous operation and navigation.

Mechanical Design & Fabrication

Iterated chassis geometry and mounting features in SolidWorks, validating component fit, wiring routes, and assembly sequence through 3D printing and physical testing. The chassis was fabricated from PLA plastic using FDM 3D printing for cost-effectiveness and structural integrity.

Enabled LiDAR-based localization and navigation using SLAM/ROS2, resulting in reliable autonomous motion that validated chassis stability and sensor integration. The project demonstrates a complete design, build, and test cycle for a robust mobile robotic platform.

3 Axis Robot Arm

SolidWorks Gearbox Mechanism Stepper Motor CAD Simulation Arduino Uno

Project Overview

Engineered a robotic arm attachment for wheelchairs, designed to empower individuals with limited grip strength in manipulating objects with ease and precision. The solution enhances autonomy and quality of life by enabling users to perform daily tasks independently.

Mechanical Design & Integration

The project required meticulous selection and integration of components: craft sticks for structural support, wire connectors for electrical connections, and an Arduino Uno as the central control unit. The gearbox mechanism, comprising gears, rods, and motors, transmits rotational motion and torque from DC motors to facilitate smooth and efficient movement of the arm's gripper mechanism. A stepper motor enables precise control and manipulation of objects, enhancing the arm's functionality and usability. CAD software was used to refine and optimize the arm's geometry, ensuring structural integrity and mechanical robustness. Simulation techniques, such as stress and displacement analysis, validated the arm's performance under operational loads.

Results & Iteration

While individual subsystems showed success, the overall prototype failed to meet project requirements due to inconsistent gearbox performance, slow gripper motion, and structural limitations. Future iterations focus on strengthening connections, optimizing motor speed, and reducing friction in the gripper system for increased efficiency and functionality.

Autonomous Mining Loader

SolidWorks PID Control Line Following ItsyBitsy Microcontroller Infrared Sensor Servo Motor

Project Overview

A fully autonomous mobile robot designed to replicate the functionalities of self-driving digging trucks used in modern mining operations. The primary goal was to demonstrate the viability of autonomous technology in enhancing efficiency and safety within mining environments.

Mechanical & Control Design

Implemented a line-following navigation algorithm utilizing a PID controller for precise line-following capabilities. The Ziegler-Nichols method was used for tuning the PID controller, enabling the robot to navigate predefined paths with high accuracy. An open-loop control handled intersections, ensuring seamless path continuation at complex junctures.

The hardware setup included an ItsyBitsy microcontroller, LED indicators, buttons, reflective object sensors, DC motors, an infrared distance sensor, and servo motors. This hardware facilitated autonomous navigation and task execution by providing real-time sensor data to the control algorithms. The integration of these components allowed MiniBot to perform its tasks efficiently, demonstrating the importance of a well-coordinated hardware-software system in achieving autonomous operation.

Results

The final prototype achieved an 80% success rate in item collection and transportation, effectively simulating the functions of autonomous digging trucks in mining applications.
