About Me

I’m Josh Westlake, a third year Mechatronics and Robotics Engineering student at Queen’s University with a strong focus on mechanical design and building real robotic systems. I enjoy taking ideas from concept to working hardware through CAD, prototyping, testing, and iteration. Most of my experience sits at the intersection of mechanics, electronics, and controls, with a particular interest in robot mechanisms, electromechanical assemblies, and systems that need to perform reliably in the real world.

This portfolio highlights projects where I’ve designed and built autonomous robots, custom mechanisms, sensor suites, and electromechanical systems. I’m actively looking for opportunities in robotics and mechanical design engineering where I can contribute hands on, learn quickly, and help turn ambitious ideas into real products.
